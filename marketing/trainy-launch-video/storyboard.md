# Trainy launch film storyboard

Runtime: **90 seconds** (2,700 frames at 30 fps)
Canvas: **3840 × 2160**, 16:9
Review render: **1920 × 1080** via a 0.5 render scale
Audio: licensed upbeat electronic score, 48 kHz stereo, no narration

The film is one continuous product journey rather than a stack of title cards.
Captured interactions run at their real 1× speed. Product cuts follow actions
inside Trainy—search, result, trip, departure board, unavailable state, retry,
accessibility, and settings—while a restrained camera system moves only among
named center, left, right, and detail poses. The direction draws on Apple’s
restraint, precision, product focus, and musical pacing without copying Apple
assets, music, copy, devices, or campaign compositions.

## Motion and framing contract

- Every captured clip plays at **1×**. Slow motion and speed ramps are not used.
- Product surfaces are shown inside a consistent glass-and-chassis treatment;
  no fake hardware render or generated device imagery is used.
- Camera motion stays between four deliberate, crop-safe poses. No camera move
  cuts through a product control or crops a simulator status area.
- Every adjacent product clip overlaps by exactly eight frames, shares the
  center pose at the cut, and keeps the simulator status bar fixed at `9:21`.
  There are no restarted source segments, rotating panels, slide wipes,
  carousel cards, or CSS-timed animations.
- The decorative journey line is strongest only before the product reveal,
  drops to 20% during Japan, 12% during Utrecht, and disappears for the resolve.
- Editorial copy remains inside generous 4K title-safe margins and never covers
  the active region of the captured UI.
- The accessibility state is intentionally brief; it proves Dark Mode and
  AX2XL support without allowing enlarged text to dominate the film.
- The score’s natural ending is preserved, followed by silence under the final
  creation credit.
